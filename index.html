About

I'm a Postdoctoral Scholar at UC Davis, working with the AHMCT Research Center and CORE Lab in the Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering. My research spans autonomous vehicle perception and control, transportation electrification, and charging-infrastructure optimization — much of it funded through Caltrans.

I hold a Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Western Michigan University and an M.Sc. from Iran University of Science and Technology. Across academia and industry, I've been drawn to the same thing: the interplay between rigor and beauty — where mathematical structure produces systems that are not only correct, but elegant.

My focus is closing the gap between research and deployment — models that survive contact with real fleets, real roads, and real weather.

02.

Research Focus

01 / perception

Autonomous Perception & Control

Eco-driving controls, sensor systems, and robust perception — including lane detection under snow-occluded conditions where conventional methods break down.

02 / fleets

Fleet Electrification

Large-scale EV fleet analysis with Geotab telematics — departure/arrival modeling, energy demand, and electrification feasibility across depot sites.

03 / infrastructure

Charging Infrastructure

Mobile DC fast charging (DCFC) siting and optimization, charger-sizing heuristics, and cost modeling for real-world deployment.

04 / modeling

Physics-Informed ML

Neural networks constrained by physical laws for EV energy-consumption prediction — combining data efficiency with physical consistency.
